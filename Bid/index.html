Introduction

This is a public call, issued by the International World Wide Web Conferences Committee (IW3C2), to bid for the publication of the yearly Web Conference proceedings. A successful bidder is expected to sign a contract with IW3C2 for a period of five years, starting in 2018. The bid should be submitted by the end of February of 2017 with the goal of signing the contract by the autumn of of 2017. The bid should include:

  • a timetable for the authors and the conference organizers (e.g., how many weeks before the start of the conference should all contributions be submitted to the publishers to have the proceedings on-line at the event);
  • itemize any fees that might be payable by the conference;
  • how the publisher would fulfill the detailed requirements, as described in a separate section below.

Bids should be submitted by email to the publishers@iw3c2.org mailing list.

Background

The Web Conference series (i.e, WWW2014, WWW2015, etc.) is the prime yearly international conference on the topic of the future directions of the World Wide Web.

The series started in 1994 at CERN, when the main creators and developers of the World Wide Web decided to hold a regular event to help moving the Web forward. The International World Wide Web Conferences Committee (IW3C2) was formed to maintain the series of events, and it has been doing it ever since. The conference series aim to provide a premier forum for discussion, debate, and research on the evolution of the Web, the standardization of its associated technologies, or the impact of those technologies on society and culture. The conference brings together researchers, developers, users, and commercial ventures—indeed all those who are passionate about the Web and what it has to offer. The peer-reviewed paper track of the conference has become one of the most prestigious venues for researchers all over the world to publish their research results in the field; the proceedings of the conference is indexed and highly ranked by all tools and organizations that measure academic performances. The conference’s location rotates among the Americas, Europe, and Australasia and its events usually span a period of five days, and takes place in the April-May periods. In the past few years the conference was held in Seoul, Florence, and Montréal; the venues for 2017 and 2018 will be Perth and Lyon, respectively. See the the list of all the conferences on the site of IW3C2.

Current Proceedings

The proceedings of the conference, containing all the peer-reviewed academic papers, includes around 120 academic papers, published in digital form only (i.e., printed proceedings are not produced). Although the conference is not an ACM conference, the publication is part of the ACM digital library. The publication is based on a contract between the ACM and IW3C2; this contract expires after the publication of the conference proceedings of the event in Perth in 2017.

The agreement with the ACM is such that the copyright for all papers is held by the IW3C2 and not by the ACM. IW3C2 has the right to publish the proceedings on its own Web site, and it indeed does that. The IW3C2 version of the proceedings is made available through an open access policy; IW3C2 publishes the articles under a CC BY 4.0 license (see the IW3C2 Copyright Release Form for further details on these arrangements).

As an example for a proceedings you can look at the proceedings of the WWW2016 conference held in Montréal.

Alternate Proceedings

The Web Conference is a 5 day event that also includes tracks that are not part of the peer-reviewed academic track. Examples are tutorials, workshops, sessions on special topics, etc. Some of these, typically workshops, have their own organizers with their own peer-review system and also intend to provide their own proceedings. Individual workshops may choose their own publication approaches, but there is an informal agreement between the ACM and IW3C2 to publish those proceedings, if the organizers of a particular workshop so desire, through the ACM. These are not part of the conferences’ main proceedings, and form what is referred to as “alternate proceedings”, often published as a “companion volume”.

This request for bids focuses on the core, technical track proceedings only. However, publishers should also tell IW3C2 whether they are open for such, possibly one-off, year-by-year agreements with individual conferences to publish companion volumes with workshops who wish to publish their proceedings following essentially the same setups as the main publication. This has been the case for several workshops for past conferences.

General Description

As referred to in the introduction, IW3C2 needs a new contract to publish the conference proceedings starting from the proceedings of WWW2018, to be held in Lyon, France, in April 2018. In order for the paper submission process of the conference to provide the right publishing information to prospective authors, a contract between a publisher and IW3C2 must be in place in the autumn of 2017. Therefore, to leave enough time for internal discussion, final proposals from publishers should be in the hand of IW3C2 by the end of February of 2017.

IW3C2 wants to use this opportunity to renew the publication of its proceedings. The proceedings of the conference should fully embrace the possibilities of the modern Web, and adapt to the new realities of scholarly publishing. This means a list of new requirements that a proceedings should fulfill in terms of its digital presence; details of these requirements are listed below. IW3C2 is of course open to intermediate discussions to discuss some of the details and provide clarifications.

Regardless of whether IW3C2 to publishes the proceedings of its own site, W3C2 may want to receive a copy of the proceedings for the purpose of long-term archival. The bid should make it clear whether this is acceptable or not.

Publishing Requirements

The requirements are grouped into three categories:

  1. Requirements for the individual papers
  2. Requirements for the proceedings as a whole
  3. Requirements for the workflow of paper submissions

For each of these categories the requirements are grouped into absolute, strong, and “nice to have”. Absolute requirements are those that IW3C2 wants to see fulfilled in any case. The other two are not necessarily deal breakers, but may differentiate bids and influence the final choice.

Requirements for Individual Papers

Absolute Requirements

  • Each paper has its own DOI registered through services like CrossRef.
  • The papers should be under open access of some sort; preferably under CC0-BY copyright.
    • In particular authors of the papers should have the right to re-use their publication, with due reference to the published version, in their research work, e.g., as part of their thesis or other publications.
    • IW3C2 may accept to change its current copyright policy by letting the publishers hold the copyright; this is open for discussions during the final negotiations.
  • Articles should be published in valid and accessible HTML5+CSS3.
    • Extra resources, like extra CSS or JavaScript that are necessary for the proper presentation of the paper and provided by the authors, as well as embedded materials like figures, images, video, or audio must be stored locally on the publisher’s system alongside the paper itself. The goal is that the papers must stay readable as submitted for perpetuity.
    • Scripts should be restricted to provide facilities essential to the publication like interactive data display; it is acceptable if these are restricted to secure sandboxes (e.g., iframe) for security reasons.
  • Articles must be downloadable in EPUB3.1, including all the media, and in PDF, possibly without the interactive media, i.e., only a print image.
    • Should EPUB evolve in a few years (possibly under another name) the publisher should be prepared to upgrade to the latest standard eventually, based on an agreement with IW3C2.
  • Easy access, via hyperlinks where applicable, to the usual metadata (title, authors with their ORCID identifiers, DOI, etc.).
  • One-click tools to publish references to Twitter, Facebook, Pocket, etc. should be integrated.
  • Usual citation exports (Zotero, Mendelay, BiBTeX, Endnote, etc.) should be provided.
  • Easy download of the article metadata in a machine readable format. IW3C2 will provide references to the desired metadata.

The exact list for the external services (Facebook, Twitter, …) and formats (Zotero, BiBTEX, …) can be discussed during the final negotiations, and may also evolve over the years.

Strong Requirements

  • Beyond the CC0-BY (or equivalent) copyright, authors of the publications should have the right to put a copy of their proceedings on their personal Web Site (possibly in the format of, and a reference to, the publisher).
  • Possibility for storage/access to the presentation slides if the authors so desire, or providing a link to other storage sites like SlideShare or Speaker Deck.
  • Downloadable metadata expressed in RDF (serialized in Turtle and/or JSON-LD).
  • Annotating/commenting facilities on the papers with the possibility for further dialogues (replies, threads, etc). Authors should be automatically notified of traffic. These features should be left open beyond the conference itself.

“Nice to Have”

  • Table of Contents and/or references accessible while reading a paper, without leaving the current view (e.g., pop up on request, side bar, etc). Same with the list of tables, figures, links to accompanying datasets, source code, etc.
  • Better and more interactive reference list handling, e.g.,
    • details of a reference entry should appear as popup when hovering on the reference in the paper;
    • references’ entries also available as data (RDF, BiBTeX) for easy incorporation to a researcher’s workflow.
  • Local storage of supplementary materials, datasets, program code, etc.
    • supplementary materials may not be available for local storage for a variety of reasons (legal, practical, etc); this requirement is on offering this facility to authors, rather than requiring the local storage.
  • Up-to-date information on citation data (e.g., reference to relevant services like Google scholar), related papers, etc.

Requirements for a Proceedings as a Whole

The main requirements for individual articles also apply to the proceedings as a whole, considered as a single publication by itself when applicable (e.g., DOI, EPUB3.1 downloadable version, proceedings‘ metadata, etc.). This section lists only the extra facilities.

Absolute Requirements

  • The proceedings should remain freely available on the Web in perpetuity, with a stable identifiers (separate DOI and, possibly, with an unchanged URL).
  • Search on the papers by authors, titles, or keywords.

Strong Requirements

  • Full text search over the papers.

“Nice to Have”

  • Semantic search (e.g., through a SPARQL interface) over the publications’ metadata.
  • Inclusion of additional media on the conference (e.g., selected photos or videos taken at the event, etc.), to reflect the less formal aspects of the event, all as part of the proceedings.

Requirements for the Workflow of Paper Submissions

Absolute Requirement

  • Authors must have the possibility to submit in HTML, MS Word (i.e., DOCX), or LaTeX using the publisher’s style sheets or equivalents. Conversion to HTML5 must be done by the publisher, including the final typesetting and formatting details.
  • Reviewers should be able to access the papers in any of the above formats according to their preferences.

Strong requirements

  • Accept additional input formats, like ODT, Apple Pages, RTF, or PDF.

“Nice to Have”

  • Provide an on-line WYSIWYG authoring tool that authors are authorized to use to produce their final publication.
